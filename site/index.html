The problem

Clinical trial teams already use ChatGPT and Claude in real workflows.

  • Staff paste SAE narratives, protocol excerpts, monitoring notes, and regulatory drafting content into consumer LLMs.
  • The risk is not just PHI. It also includes protocol IP, safety findings, and market-sensitive trial information.
  • Current defenses are weak tradeoffs: blocking hurts workflow, policy alone is ignored, and simple redaction often destroys utility.

Why this matters

Clinical trials are a uniquely good target for privacy mediation.

  • Documents are standardized enough to model rigorously.
  • Workflows are identifiable: medical writing, PV, CRA review, protocol operations.
  • Sensitivity is mixed: patient privacy plus confidential trial strategy.

Evaluation

Privacy is treated as an attack problem, not a marketing claim.

Verbatim leak

Checks whether any sensitive span survives literally or fuzzily in the proxy.

Similarity retrieval

Measures whether the proxy remains semantically close enough for retrieval-based linkage.

Trained inversion

Tests whether an attacker can recover sensitive spans from proxy text alone.

Membership inference

Estimates whether a target entity can be inferred from the mediated output.

Utility regression

Measures whether the cloud answer remains useful relative to sending the raw input.

Closing idea

The question is not whether AI will be used. It is what can safely leave the machine.

NGSP reframes LLM privacy from access control to controlled transformation for high-stakes clinical trial workflows.
